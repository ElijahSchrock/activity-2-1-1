Directions

  1. Take a look at W3School's page about quotes and citations. These are elements you may not have seen yet.

    • Which tag is used for short quotes?

      • The tag for short quotes is <q>

    • Which tag is used for longer quotes?

      • <blockquote>

    • Where does the citation go when quoting a long text?

      • The <cite> goes after the </blockquote>

    • Which tag is used for abbreviations?

      • <abbr>

    • What does the bdo tag do?

      • The <bdo> is used to override the current direction of the text and change the direction of the text.

  2. Take a look at MDN's page on img tags.

    • What browsers support JPEG files by default?

      • All versions of Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Internet Explorer, Opera, and Safari

    • What browsers support TIFF files by default?

      • Safari

    • What happens with an img tag if the src attribute is left empty?

      • Nothing will happen and the image wil not appear

    • What does the srcset attribute do?

      • <srcset> defines the set of images we will allow the browser to choose between, and what size each image is

  3. Take a look at MDN's article on the details element. Note: You may not have seen this tag before!

    • Take a look at the code example. What do you think is the purpose of such an element?

      • <details> is used to create a tab that includes details you don't want displayed loudly

    • What purpose does the summary element serve?

      • <summary> is used to give the tab a name
